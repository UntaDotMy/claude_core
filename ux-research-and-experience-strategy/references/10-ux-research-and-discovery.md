# UX Research and Discovery

## Method Selection

Pick methods by decision type:

- Exploratory discovery: contextual inquiry, interviews, diary studies.
- Concept validation: prototype tests, moderated sessions.
- Behavioral measurement: analytics funnel analysis, surveys, A/B experiments.
- Service/process validation: journey walkthroughs, service blueprint reviews, support-log analysis.

## Research Planning

- Define hypotheses and key questions first.
- Define participant criteria and recruitment strategy.
- Define data capture plan and ethics/privacy boundaries.
- Define success criteria for accepting/rejecting assumptions.

## Sampling and Bias Controls

- Ensure target-representative sampling where possible.
- Watch for self-selection and confirmation bias.
- Triangulate findings across multiple data sources.
- Document confidence level and known blind spots.
- Capture segment differences (novice vs expert users, device contexts, accessibility needs).

## Research Outputs

Deliver findings as:

- Evidence-backed themes
- Severity-ranked pain points
- Opportunity statements
- Recommended actions with expected user impact
- Explicit “do now vs later” prioritization tied to delivery risk

## Research Ethics

### Informed Consent
- Explain what you're testing and why
- Explain how data will be used
- Get explicit consent to record
- Allow participants to withdraw anytime

### Privacy
- Anonymize participant data
- Secure storage of recordings/notes
- Don't share identifying information
- Follow GDPR/privacy regulations

### Respect
- Don't make participants feel stupid
- Thank them for their time
- Compensate fairly
- Act on their feedback

# UX Scale, Governance, and Collaboration

## Scaling UX Across Teams

- Standardize reusable interaction patterns and content patterns.
- Document rationale for UX decisions, not only final screens.
- Align UX practices with product and engineering delivery cycles.

## Governance Model

- Define UX review checkpoints at discovery, design, pre-release, and post-release.
- Define ownership for UX debt and consistency issues.
- Track design system adoption and deviation exceptions.
- Require decision logs for major UX trade-offs and accepted risks.

## Cross-Functional Collaboration

- Pair UX, product, and engineering during problem framing.
- Include QA in usability and accessibility validation plans.
- Share research findings broadly to reduce duplicate discovery work.
- Route final implementation through reviewer gates before release when UX impact is material.

## Enterprise Readiness

- Include accessibility and localization as non-negotiable requirements.
- Include privacy and trust implications in UX decisions.
- Use objective UX quality indicators in release readiness decisions.
- Track follow-up UX debt with owners and target release windows.

## Team Best Practices

1. Talk to real users; don't assume
2. Test early and often; don't wait for perfect designs
3. Small sample sizes (5-8 users) find most usability issues
4. Observe behavior over what users say
5. Ask why to understand mental models
6. Prioritize ruthlessly; fix high-impact issues first
7. Measure impact before and after changes
8. Iterate continuously; UX is never "done"
9. Collaborate closely with design and engineering
10. Share insights broadly to reduce duplicate discovery
11. Protect brownfield familiarity unless evidence proves it's the problem
12. Validate in the real interface when behavior details are part of the risk

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Testing with internal team instead of real users
- Leading questions that bias responses
- Testing too late (after implementation)
- Ignoring negative feedback
- Not prioritizing findings
- Vague recommendations without specifics
- Testing without clear objectives
- Not following up to measure impact
- Assuming you represent the user
- Over-designing based on edge cases
